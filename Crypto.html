Types of Cryptocurrency


Bitcoin

1 Bitcoin = 47,44,019.26 Indian Rupee
Bitcoin (₿) is a decentralized digital currency, without a central bank or single administrator, that can be sent from user to user on the peer-to-peer bitcoin network without the need for intermediaries.

Cardano

1 Cardano = 165.8 Indian Rupee
Cardano is a public blockchain platform. It is open-source and decentralized, with consensus achieved using proof of stake. Cardano was founded in 2015 by Ethereum co-founder Charles Hoskinson.

Dogecoin

1 Dogecoin = 18.48 Indian Rupee
Dogecoin is a cryptocurrency created by software engineers Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer, who decided to create a payment system as a "joke", making fun of the wild speculation in cryptocurrencies at the time.

Ethereum

1 Ethereum = 2,88,584.38 Indian Rupee
Ethereum is a decentralized, open-source blockchain with smart contract functionality. Ether is second only to Bitcoin in market capitalization. Ethereum was conceived in 2013 by programmer Vitalik Buterin.

Litecoin

1 Litecoin = 13,991.68 Indian Rupee
Litecoin is a peer-to-peer cryptocurrency and open-source software project released under the MIT/X11 license. Litecoin was an early bitcoin spinoff or altcoin, starting in October 2011.

About us

Cryptocurrency is a type of digital currency that
generally only exists electronically.

but there are many different cryptocurrency brands,

do people use cryptocurrency?

People use cryptocurrency for quick payments, to avoid transaction fees that regular banks charge, or because it offers some anonymity. Others hold cryptocurrency as an investment, hoping the value goes up.
Where and how do you store cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is stored in a digital wallet, which can be online, on your computer, or on an external hard drive.

How do you get cryptocurrency?

You can buy cryptocurrency through an online exchange platform. Some people earn cryptocurrency through a complex process called mining.
How is cryptocurrency different from flat money?

Cryptocurrencies exist in digital form as they are created by computers and operate as private pieces of code. The means of exchange is purely digital. In contrast fiat money can be both digital or physical.
